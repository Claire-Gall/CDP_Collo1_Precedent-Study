Rafael Lozano-Hemmer: Profile
Subversive Technology for Ephemeral Focal Points in Architecture
Rafael Lozano-Hemmer
Project Format
Public Installation, Multi-Media Art, Database Development, Live Visitor Engagement, Radio Frequencing
"Under Scan", Relational Architecture 15, 2008
Location
Plaza de las Tres Culturas, Mexico City, México

Voz Alta (Loud Voice) (Relational Architecture 15) is a live installation memorializing a student massacre that took place in Tlatelolco, Mexico, on October 2nd, 1968. Commissioned by UNAM University, Rafael Lozano-Hemmer designed this functional prototype of a megaphone that amplifies people's voices as powerful beams of light at the site of the massacre. Archival materials were emitted through the megaphone along with live visitor statements as beams cascaded over Mexico City in cadence with their voices. Lozano-Hemmer has always been deeply curious about precedents of technological art, including the Estridentista poets, who helped launch radio broadcasting in 1920s Mexico.

Technologies and Techniques Used

4×10kW Xenon robotic searchlights, modified megaphone, computers, DMX distribution, live FM radio transmission

"Under Scan", Relational Architecture 11, 2005

Under Scan (Relational Architecture 11) uses surveillance technology as a subversive technique, creating vantage points that offer visitors novel perspectives on their surroundings and potential touchpoints for connection.

This piece preceded Voz Alta and was activated throughout public spaces in the United Kingdom.
